What is mental health?

Mental health includes emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, and act — and how we handle stress, relate to others, and make choices in life.

+
💛

Why does it matter?

Good mental health enables us to realise our potential, cope with life's stresses, work productively, and contribute to our communities. It is the foundation of overall wellbeing.

+
⚡

Common challenges

Anxiety, depression, burnout, and loneliness are more common than most realise. Recognising early signs is the most powerful thing you can do for yourself or someone you love.

+
🌱

What actually helps?

Sleep, movement, meaningful connection, and talking openly — whether to a friend, therapist, or AI companion — are all evidence-based paths to better mental wellbeing.

Myth vs. fact
Myth

Mental health problems are rare and only affect a small number of people.

Fact

1 in 5 adults experience a mental health condition every year. It is extremely common.

Myth

People with mental health issues are weak or just need to "toughen up."

Fact

Mental health conditions are medical issues influenced by biology, environment, and life experience — not character flaws.

Myth

Mental health conditions are permanent and there's nothing you can do.

Fact

With the right support and treatment, most people significantly improve and lead fulfilling lives.

Myth

Talking about mental health makes things worse.

Fact

Talking openly reduces stigma, builds connection, and is one of the most effective tools for healing.
